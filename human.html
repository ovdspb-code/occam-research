About this visualization

T2 — Basal Ganglia Gating Loop: Sources are striatal nuclei (Caudate, Putamen, Accumbens). Targets are thalamus and pallidum — the gating output. CRN predicts that intermediate dephasing (κ) optimizes selective signal routing. T3 — Motor Relay: Thalamocortical broadcast pathway where structure dominates and weight-tuning has minimal effect. Compare the two to see the architectural dissociation.

T2 vs T3 — Architectural Dissociation

Both ports exhibit the inverted-U transport profile (ENAQT), but they respond differently to weight perturbation. In T2, shuffling edge weights destroys selectivity (G > 0) — the specific weight pattern matters. In T3, surrogates perform equally well (G ≈ 0) — topology alone is sufficient. This double dissociation is a core prediction of CRN: gating circuits require weight-tuned interference, while relay circuits broadcast via graph geometry.
